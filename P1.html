IUCN(page1)

The World Conservation Union (International Union for Conservation of Nature), or IUCN,, is the largest and oldest global non-profit environmental organization in the world, and the only international organization in the field of natural environment protection and sustainable development as a permanent observer of the United Nations General Assembly. Founded in 1948 in Fontainebleau, France (Fontainebleau), headquartered in Gran, Switzerland, also serves as the "International Union for the Conservation of Nature and Natural Resources ".

IUCN is a unique global alliance, one of the few international organizations in which governmental and non-governmental institutions can participate, with member organizations divided into sovereign States and non-profit institutions, while expert committees accept individuals as volunteer members. There are currently over 200 members of national and governmental institutions from 161 countries and over 1,000 members of non-governmental institutions; more than 16000 individual members of scholars have joined the Committee of Experts. IUCN now has offices in nearly 50 countries, with more than 1,000 employees. The current Chairman is Mr. Zhang Xinsheng of China and the Director-General is Ms. Ingle Anderson of Denmark.
